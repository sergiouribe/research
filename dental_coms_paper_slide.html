DentalCOMS: Core Outcome Measures for Computer Vision

Citation: Büttner M, Rokhshad R, Brinz J, Issa J, Chaurasia A, Uribe SE, Karteva T, Chala S, Tichy A, Schwendicke F. Core outcomes measures in dental computer vision studies (DentalCOMS). Journal of Dentistry. 2024;150:105318.
Research Question: Can we develop standardized core outcome measures to improve reporting and reduce bias in dental computer vision studies?
Answer: Yes. Eight core outcome measures were agreed upon through e-Delphi consensus with 26 international experts from WHO/ITU/WIPO AI4H initiative. Human expert assessment is the only feasible method for clinically meaningful evaluation.

Key Findings

Current Problem

  • 42 different diagnostic accuracy measures used across dental AI studies
  • Non-systematic choice driven by researcher preferences
  • Studies not comparable, limited synthesis options
  • High risk of selective reporting bias

Consensus Process

  • 26 participants from IADR, WHO/ITU/WIPO AI4H, ADEA, AAOMFR
  • 70% agreement threshold (scale 1-10, agreement ≥7)
  • Two-round e-Delphi process
  • Stable agreement achieved after first round

Eight Core Outcome Measures (DentalCOMS)

Confusion Matrix

Visual representation of true/false predictions

Accuracy

Proportion of total correct predictions

Sensitivity

Proportion of lesions correctly identified

Specificity

Proportion of healthy cases correctly identified

Precision (PPV)

Exactness of positive predictions

F1-Score

Harmonic mean of precision and sensitivity

AUC-ROC

Area under receiver operating characteristic curve

AUC-PR

Area under precision-recall curve

Critical Insight: Different computer vision tasks (classification, segmentation, detection) require different evaluation approaches. Pixel-level metrics often lack clinical relevance. Human expert assessment remains essential for clinically meaningful evaluation.

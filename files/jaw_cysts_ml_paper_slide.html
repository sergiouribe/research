Emulating Clinical Diagnostic Reasoning for Jaw Cysts with ML

Citation: Feher B, Kuchler U, Schwendicke F, Schneider L, Cejudo Grano de Oro JE, Xi T, Vinayahalingam S, Hsu TMH, Brinz J, Chaurasia A, et al. Emulating Clinical Diagnostic Reasoning for Jaw Cysts with Machine Learning. Diagnostics. 2022;12(8):1968.
Research Question: Can machine learning emulate the human clinical diagnostic reasoning process for detecting and classifying jaw cysts using spatial relationships to anatomical structures?
Answer: Yes. A combined object detection and image segmentation approach successfully emulated clinical reasoning by first detecting cysts, then analyzing spatial relationships to anatomical structures, achieving performance comparable to human clinicians.

Dataset Characteristics

  • 1,239 panoramic radiographs total
  • 855 OPGs for training (all positives)
  • 384 OPGs for testing (38% prevalence)
  • Multicenter: Vienna + Nijmegen
  • Histopathological confirmation required

Novel Approach

  • RetinaNet object detection (cyst location)
  • U-Net++ segmentation (anatomical structures)
  • Random Forest classifier (overlap analysis)
  • Spatial relationship analysis mimics clinical reasoning
Critical Findings:
• AI performed better than humans for odontogenic cyst detection (F1: 0.86 vs 0.56)
• Human inter-rater agreement was poor (κ = 0.28)
• Object detection achieved average precision of 0.42 and recall of 0.39
• Classification performance remained stable despite imperfect cyst detection
Clinical Significance: First study to deliberately replicate multi-step clinical reasoning process. Spatial relationships to anatomical structures (tooth proximity to radicular cysts) provided strong diagnostic information, validating clinical diagnostic approach.
